Watson Lab Behavioral Box : Cleaning water lines

Created by Brendon Omar Watson, last modified on Jun 29, 2020
  1. Clean sucrose syringe
    1. Take out sucrose syringe by putting in plunger, picking up entire syringe out of holder, inverting syringe then taking out luer lock
    2. Go to sink
    3. Dump contents
    4. Rinse a couple of times w clean water
  2. Use this newly clean syringe to clean the line that came down from that syringe
    1. Detatch that line from solenoid
    2. Hook top to new clean syringe
    3. Run clean water through over sink 2-3 times
  3. Re-place these in their usual places and hook up to solenoid
  4. For sucrose line: clean bottom line emerging out from solenoid as well
    1. Take bottom line off of solenoid
    2. May want to pull dispenser syringe out of beambreak
    3. Place paper towel or similar to catch water that will come out of dispenser
    4. Attach a new syringe to this and push clean water through
    5. Hook back up so entire line is now back intact 
  5. Test sucrose line
    1. After all is hooked back up
    2. Place ~3ml water in syringe at top
    3. Put plunger into syringe and apply a bit of pressure on the syringe/water/system
    4. Trigger dispense multiple times w beambreak - should see spurt of water if under pressure
    5. Repeat until all water is washed through
    6. To remove plunger, keep triggering beambreak so that solenoid opens allowing pressure to release as plunger is pulled back
    7. IF THIS DOESN'T WORK - open the line longer term using this: Arduino-based Diagnostics Procedure
  6. Test clean water line
    1. Repeat steps 5
