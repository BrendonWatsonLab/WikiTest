Watson Lab Behavioral Box : Power Distribution Board Assembly

Created by Paul Joachim Fitzgerald, last modified by Anjesh Ghimire on May 21, 2021

Summary from I.Cerda Thesis:

One half a red Breadboard Mini takes in 12 V of DC input from the power supply and distributes it to an Arduino Mega 2564, a buck converter stepping down voltage for the Motor Shield Assembly, and a relay board controlling lighting in the box. The second half contains the Water Solenoid Valve Driving Circuit, which regulates the opening of solenoid valves for water dispensing.



Power distribution board assembly

  • Section 1: Solenoid Control Components
  • Section 2: Power Rail Components


Section 1: Solenoid Control Components

These components occupy approximately half of the Red Solderable Breadboard Mini and serve to control the power to the two Solenoids used by the Water Subsystem.

  1. Components needed
    1. 1, Red Solderable Breadboard Mini
    2. 2, 10 kOhm resistors
    3. 2, black and amber (zener) diodes
    4. 2, mosfets (tube of these has blue ends)
      1. One end of each mosfet is bridged, the other not
    5. 4, Male 3-Pin Connector
  2. “sparkfun” side of breadboard mini should face upward
  3. Insert both mosfets on breadboard mini (in correct orientation)
  4. Tin the tip of the soldering iron
  5. Do not hold the soldering iron on mosfets for very long, to avoid heating them too much
  6. Switch between mosfet pins while soldering to minimize heating of them
  7. Attach resistors to breadboard mini
    1. First bend their legs
    2. Push into breadboard mini
    3. Solder in place
    4. Clip off excess legs
  8. Attach 2 black and amber (zener) diodes
    1. Black stripe faces mosfet
    2. Bend legs (be gentle since it’s delicate)
    3. Push into breadboard mini
    4. Solder in place
      1. Do not heat for too long
    5. Clip off excess legs
  9. Modify 2 of the 3 pin male connectors
    1. Remove white wire from each
      1. Push down with small flathead screwdriver on metal contact a
      2. Pull out white wire
    2. Strip away ends of red and green wires to expose more conductor
    3. Solder them onto breadboard mini
  10. Strip ends and solder unmodified 3 pin male connectors onto breadboard mini
