Watson Lab Behavioral Box : Motorshield Custom Bottom Board

Created by Pho Hale, last modified on Oct 15, 2019

The Motorshield Custom Bottom Board is a strip of MAS-Pro Stripboard that has been snapped down to the right size to be attached to the bottom of the Motorshield Assembly and serve as the positive (5V) and ground (GND) rails for the Female 3-Pin Connectors that connect to the beambreaks and the solenoid power board.



Procedure:

  1. Draw black "break" lines across the columns with the following letters: {D,H,L,P,T}.
  2. Insert the MAS-Pro Stripboard into the large vise as depicted, ensuring that the vise's edge is aligned with the black line you drew in step 1.
  3. Put on a work glove, and push with your palm on the bottom of the board, where it meets the vise. The goal is to ensure a clean break.
  4. Repeat the process for the other lines. You should obtain 6 Motorshield Custom Bottom Boards from a single MAS-Pro Stripboard if you do it without errors.
  5. For the next step, be sure that you and anyone working even remotely near you has on Safety Goggles. This is mandatory, as the parts will fly off very rapidly when you cut them.
  6. Cut the ends of the board off as shown using the clippers. Note that in the picture I'm grasping the board with the pliers so they don't break. This isn't necessary, but is helpful if you encounter trouble with the board fracturing. I prefer to cut off the end without the letter labels, and to cut as near to the holes as possible without damaging them.
