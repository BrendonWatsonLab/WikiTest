Watson Lab Behavioral Box : Motorshield Assembly

Created by Paul Joachim Fitzgerald, last modified by Pho Hale on Mar 02, 2020






Motor shield assembly (watched and photographed Pho 10/4/2019)


  1. MAS-Pro Stripboard
  2. Header pins
    1. Break ones that do NOT come with motor shield into 2 strips of 4
      1. We buy these separately
    2. The longer header pins that do come with motor shield are also broken into 4 longer stretches to sandwich with the Arduino


***Pho recommends using lead solder for all assembly of box components.  But always wash hands after using lead-based solder.


  1. Solder long stretches of header pins into place
    1. Solder pins on ends of each of the 4 stretches first, trying to keep perpendicular to board
    2. Test if Arduino plugs in before soldering the rest
    3. If it fits, then solder the rest of the pins (which cannot be bridged)


  1. Cut MAS-Pro Stripboard with box cutter
    1. Want to retain 3 intact rows, so cut through middle of fourth row several times
    2. Flip over and do the same
    3. Load into vise directly up to cut line
    4. Put on work gloves
    5. Apply pressure from your palm (at bottom of exposed board) to break
    6. Can cut and break off a second piece as a backup
    7. Trim (green) end of stripboard with scissors


  1. Attach cut piece of stripboard to motor shield
    1. Use the 2 strips of 4 header pins
    2. The column of first 3 holes of stripboard is aligned with first hole of motor shield (right end)
    3. Apply tape (pink) to hold in header pins that will attach the stripboard to the motor shield
    4. Solder all 4 header pins at one end, then the other end
    5. Run a wire (red, 2” or less long) underneath motor shield (can use thinner wire, such as one from the “rainbow strip” of wire, than the one pictured here)
    6. Strip both ends of wire
    7. Put one end in 5V side and solder in place on the bottom row of stripboard
    8. Cut off a slightly longer (2”) green wire
    9. Solder one end to ground
    10. Solder other end of green wire to top row of stripboard (any hole)


  1. Solder 6, Female 3-Pin Connectors onto motor shield
    1. Unique white wire from each is soldered to one of the holes in the motor shield (D3, D5, D6, D7, D8, D11; which white wire goes where does not matter)
    2. The green wires from these connectors should be soldered anywhere on ground row
    3. The red wires should be soldered anywhere on 5V row
    4. Need to strip ends of white, green and red wires (to expose more conductor) to solder them
    5. It is okay to bridge red or green wires within their row with solder
    6. Cut off excess ends of soldered wires

Stepper Motors:

For a given stepper Motor there are two coils inside, each needing 2 output wires (for a total of 4 output wires)

Each pair of wires belongs to the left/right end of a single coil inside the motor called a "phase".

The wires must be grouped appropriately for the stepper to work, but the order within a group determine the direction of stepper motor rotation (clockwise/counterclockwise).
